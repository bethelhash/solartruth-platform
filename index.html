Client Documentation · v1.0.0

How SolarScope IQ Works

Every number in this tool is calculated from a named primary source — NREL, EPA, IRS, or a peer-reviewed standard. This guide explains the methodology, how to verify outputs, and what each module means for your property.

The Five-Step Pipeline

When you enter an address, the tool runs five independent analysis modules in sequence. Each module uses different data sources and produces independent outputs that are then combined into the financial summary.

① Rooftop Detection & Solar Suitability

The API locates your building in OpenStreetMap using the coordinates derived from your address. It measures the building polygon using the Shoelace formula (error <0.3% for rooftop-scale polygons), then applies NREL usable-area fractions by building type and a 1-metre fire-access setback per IFC 2021 §1504.3 to calculate net usable roof area.

Solar yield is calculated using NREL PVWatts V8 — the same model used by the US Department of Energy. It applies climate-zone specific yield (kWh/kWp/yr), shading derating, orientation factor, and a 0.86 system-loss factor to produce annual AC output in kWh.

The Suitability Score (0–100) is a weighted index: 30% area utility, 30% shading, 25% orientation, 15% climate yield. A score above 80 is excellent; 60–80 is viable; below 60 requires site review.

② Solar ROI & Financial Model

System cost uses NREL ATB 2024 tiered $/kW-DC installed costs. The Federal ITC (30% base, IRA 2022 §48E) is applied to gross CAPEX. State incentives are sourced from DSIRE (2024) and cited to their statutory source.

The 25-year financial model uses a 0.5%/yr degradation rate (Jordan & Kurtz 2013), 1.5%/yr O&M (NREL ATB 2024), and a 6% discount rate. NPV, IRR, and LCOE are calculated per IRENA (2023) methodology.

Utility rates come from EIA Form 861 (2023) — real tariffs from 60+ utilities. Net metering policy (NEM 1.0/2.0/3.0) is modelled per state using CPUC and DSIRE filings.

③ Battery Storage (BESS)

Battery size is determined via Load Duration Curve methodology (Neubauer & Simpson 2015, NREL/TP-5400-63162). CAPEX uses the NREL ATB 2024 two-component formula: battery pack ($/kWh × duration) + balance of system ($/kW). Three scenarios (low/mid/high) are shown.

LCOS (Levelised Cost of Storage) is validated against PNNL-33283 (Mongird et al. 2022) — the industry standard for storage cost benchmarking. Round-trip efficiency is 85% AC-AC for LFP (NREL ATB 2024).

The BESS ITC applies at 30% per IRA 2022 §48E when co-located with solar and charged >75% from renewable sources.

④ Building Performance Standards (BPS)

The tool screens your building against up to 7 US jurisdictions simultaneously. EUI (Energy Use Intensity in kBtu/sf/yr) is estimated from your monthly energy cost using EPA Portfolio Manager CBECS 2018 median EUI benchmarks by property type.

Penalty calculations use each jurisdiction's official carbon factor and penalty rate schedules:

NYC LL97 — Admin Code §28-320 Boston BERDO 2.0 — §7-2.2 Energize Denver — §§4-38 WA CBPS — RCW 19.27A Oregon BPS — HB 3409 Maryland BEPS — COMAR 26.28 DC BEPS — Clean Energy Omnibus Act
⑤ EV Fleet Electrification

Fleet charging load is modelled using NREL EVI-Pro 2023 Lite Model (DOE AFDC 2024). Peak demand impact is calculated per SAE J1772:2023 charger standards. Vehicle purchase credits use IRA §45W ($7,500 per qualifying commercial EV). EVSE infrastructure credits use IRA §30C (30% of equipment cost).

Federal ITC rate — Confirm at irs.gov and the DSIRE database at dsireusa.org.
✓
CAPEX range — Compare against NREL ATB 2024 (atb.nrel.gov) under Commercial PV. Tool uses tiered $/kW-DC.
✓
LL97 penalty — Use NYC's official LL97 calculator with your actual annual kWh and therms for exact figures.
✓
